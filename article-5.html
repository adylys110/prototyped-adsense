Quantum Computing: Separating Hype from Near-Term Reality

David Okafor
Deep Tech Correspondent | Published: Oct 27, 2023

Headlines promise quantum computers that will break encryption and cure diseases overnight. The reality inside research labs is far more incremental, but no less important for enterprises planning a decade ahead.

Where Quantum Actually Helps Today

Current-generation quantum hardware excels at narrow simulation problems — modeling molecular structures for drug discovery, optimizing logistics networks, and stress-testing financial risk models faster than classical supercomputers.

  • Pharmaceutical molecule simulation for faster drug candidates.
  • Portfolio optimization under complex constraint sets.
  • Materials science research for next-generation batteries.

The Error-Correction Bottleneck

Qubits remain fragile and error-prone. Most experts agree that fault-tolerant, general-purpose quantum computing capable of breaking modern encryption is still a decade or more away, giving enterprises time to prepare rather than panic.

What Businesses Should Do Now

Rather than waiting for a breakthrough, forward-looking firms are experimenting with quantum-inspired algorithms on classical hardware — a practical bridge similar to how AI-driven supply chain models matured before full automation arrived.
