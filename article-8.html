Cloud Repatriation: Why Some Enterprises Are Leaving the Cloud

Sarah Chen
Lead Cybersecurity Editor | Published: Oct 30, 2023

After a decade of "cloud-first" mandates, a growing number of enterprises are quietly moving predictable, high-volume workloads back to owned data centers — a trend known as cloud repatriation.

The Cost Curve Reversal

Cloud economics favor elastic, unpredictable workloads. For steady-state, high-volume workloads, on-demand pricing eventually exceeds the cost of owned hardware, especially once egress fees are factored in.

  • Predictable workloads often become cheaper on owned infrastructure over 3-5 years.
  • Data egress fees remain a major hidden cost of multi-cloud strategies.
  • Regulatory data residency requirements favor localized control.

Not a Full Retreat

Most repatriation is selective rather than total, echoing the same localized-processing logic behind private 5G and edge deployments — enterprises want control over specific workloads, not a wholesale exit from the cloud.

The Hybrid Future

Expect hybrid infrastructure — cloud for elastic bursts, owned hardware for steady baseline load — to remain the dominant enterprise architecture for years to come.
