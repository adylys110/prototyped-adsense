The SaaS Pricing Revolution: Usage-Based Models Explained

John Doe
Senior Tech Analyst | Published: Oct 29, 2023

Flat-rate SaaS subscriptions are losing ground to usage-based pricing, where customers pay for consumption rather than seat count. The shift is redefining how software vendors forecast revenue and how buyers budget for tools.

Why Vendors Are Making the Switch

Usage-based pricing aligns vendor revenue directly with customer value received, reducing churn friction and enabling smaller companies to adopt enterprise-grade tools at a lower entry cost.

  • Lower barriers to entry for small and mid-sized businesses.
  • Revenue scales naturally as customer usage grows.
  • Reduced incentive for "shelfware" — unused seats sitting idle.

The Budgeting Challenge

For finance teams, unpredictable usage-based bills complicate forecasting, a challenge not unlike the volatility enterprises faced when first adopting ESG-linked financial instruments with variable scoring criteria.

What to Watch in 2025

Expect hybrid models — a base platform fee plus metered usage — to become the dominant structure across mid-market and enterprise SaaS contracts.
